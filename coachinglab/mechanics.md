# Beach volleyball mechanics reference

Vetted mechanical conventions for use in coaching content. Each entry cites at least one external authoritative source. Reasoning from first principles is not sufficient — coaching mechanics have specific documented conventions, and reasoning produces plausible-sounding errors.

This file is evergreen. Add entries as mechanics get vetted; never assert a mechanic here without a source.

## Block role assignment

Two strategies. They're different systems, not points on a spectrum.

### Designated blocker

One player is the team's blocker for the whole match (selected by height, reach, net skill). The same player blocks every rally regardless of who served or who just attacked. Roles do not flip. The other player is the defender for the whole match.

### Shared blocking (split blocking)

Roles flip rally to rally. The convention differs by phase:

**Serve-receive:** the non-server blocks. The server defends. The non-server can be positioned at the net before the rally starts; the server is deep after their serve motion.

**Transition (after the team's own attack):** the player who just attacked blocks on the next play. They landed at the net after their jump and are already in position. The non-attacker (typically the setter from the previous play) was positioned back and becomes the defender.

**Underlying principle (both cases):** whichever player ends up closer to the net after the team's last action blocks. Serve-receive is the pre-arranged version; transition is the live-ball version.

## Legal vs. illegal soft attacks

**Illegal:** open-hand tip / dink using finger action (touching with the pads of the fingers and pushing). It is a fault when an attack-hit is completed with open-handed finger action or with fingertips that are not rigid and together.

**Standard non-spike attacks (what you'll actually see):**
- **Roll shot** — clean hit with the heel/palm; the ball rolls off, not paused
- **Pokey** — poke with knuckles

**Other legal options (less common in practice):**
- **Cobra** — poke with locked, straight, stiff fingertips. A curiosity rather than a standard tool.
- **Back of hand** — wrist-to-knuckles contact

Implication for coaching content: never write "tip" or "dink" as a thing a hitter does in beach. The legal equivalent is roll shot or cobra. When describing the effect a blocker has on a hitter, the right phrasing is "forced off a clean swing" or "forced into a roll shot / cobra."
